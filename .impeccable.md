## Design Context

### Users

Engineering researchers, graduate students, and technical authors preparing or auditing
manuscripts for IEEE and ACM venues. They use the project to keep claims traceable to
verified formulations, code, data, experiments, and literature while separating scientific
support from venue-specific formatting.

### Brand Personality

Rigorous, authoritative, and editorial. The experience should evoke scholarly confidence
and disciplined review without implying prestige that the underlying evidence has not earned.

### Aesthetic Direction

A refined editorial system built around deep navy, warm parchment, and restrained gold.
The visual language combines archival publication details, precise typographic rules, and
subtle evidence-chain motifs. Avoid neon technology styling, generic AI gradients, excessive
glow, glassmorphism, and unofficial imitation of IEEE or ACM corporate marks.

### Design Principles

1. Make evidence provenance visible through ordered, legible visual relationships.
2. Use gold as a scarce editorial accent, not as decoration on every element.
3. Prefer strong typographic hierarchy, generous negative space, and precise rules.
4. Convey authority through restraint; never imply guarantees the project does not provide.
5. Preserve accessibility with high contrast, readable type, and meaningful alternative text.
